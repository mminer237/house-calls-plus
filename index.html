Diabetic Help to Go

Overview

Too often DIABETES is treated like an illness in which you can just take a pill and forget it. It is really like a lifestyle. What you eat, what you drink, what you do, and your medications all affect your diabetic health. Your Daily Life. If you eat sugar, meals, or drinks with sugar. If you exercise or not, if your job is physically challenging, if you sleep a great deal, if you miss your medications, or inadvertently take too much all affects your daily health LIFE. Well, we're here to help you in real time to effect a change or guide to improve you diabetic Life. Maybe even see yourself clear of it. Let's begin.

Goals

We can begin by first recognizing your truths in the statements above. You must begin here because it will be used to you to first identify areas in which you find challenging. Then together we devise plan and implement strategies which work for you. We understand that only you can identify problem areas, but more importantly, only you hold the answers to a strategy that will work for you. This is because as I've been saying, this is your Life. You make choices moment to moment which affect your LIFE, diabetic life.

  1. Let’s begin. What is most important to you? A). I would like to be able to eat more of the foods I like. OR
  2. I would like exercise to be a part of my daily routine?
  3. Of course you already know and have been told a balance of these are needed to be healthy and well with any condition or Life circumstances.
  4. We are merely ranking your priorities so that we may be the most successful. Fighting your innate desires from the onset can only lead to failure even if we have a moment of success.

Specifications

Within this platform we will offer you. Inspiration, Motivation, Recipes, Health instruction, Exercises, and a community and individual communication space. You can select your level of involvement; weekly, monthly, or yearly. All programs have access to daily supports to use at your leisure.

Milestones

  1. Living with Diabetes

    This forum is largely for those newly diagnosed with diabetes, or those that have been told they may need to be on some form of medication for the rest of their life because they no longer produce insulin. I have grouped you together because I feel these two groups are the most creative or should allow for the most creativity. The newcomer because you need to explore options in both getting your diabetes under control and exploring the ideas of removing your diabetic label through diet and exercise or implementation of creative devices which healthily lower your blood sugars. The seasoned diabetic fits into this category because your life more than ever requires creativity. Putting a new spin on recipes, finding out that I enjoy yoga, or rock climbing, which lowers my HgA1c to my desired goals.

  2. Transitional Diabetic

    This category is for the seasoned diabetic who has found a plan with is working. The numbers are looking good. I have found a exercise or activity schedule which works for me. I can eat out, I can visit friends and family. I am in control when I have a few set backs I know how to fix them.

  3. Diabetes at Bay

    At this stage my numbers say I’m no longer a diabetic. I’m living my life medication free. I have the right balance of activities and food choices to make this work. DIabetes is at bay. I don’t have to worry about diet and exercise. My Diabetic life is working, I have the right blend to live my Life.
